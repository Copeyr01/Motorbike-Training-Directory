UK motorbike licence guide

Every stage from CBT to a full licence, what each one lets you ride, and which Edinburgh schools teach it.

CBT A1 A2 Full A Mod 1 & 2
CBT

Compulsory Basic Training

CBT is the starting point for every rider. It's a one-day practical course, not a test with a pass/fail grade — you complete it once you and the instructor agree you're safe to ride solo.

  • Minimum age: 16 for a 50cc moped, 17 for up to 125cc
  • What it lets you do: Ride up to 125cc (11kW) on the road with L-plates, unaccompanied — but no motorways, no pillion passengers
  • Validity: Your certificate (DL196) is valid for 2 years. Pass your theory and practical test within that time, or you'll need to repeat CBT

Schools that teach it, cheapest first:

  • Two Wheels Motorcycle Training — from £89
  • Harley's Rider Training — from £95
  • Edinburgh Motorcycle Training — from £99
A1

Light motorcycle licence

A1 is a full licence for a light motorcycle — no L-plates, and you can carry a passenger and use motorways, but the bike stays small.

  • Minimum age: 17
  • What it lets you do: Ride motorcycles up to 125cc and 11kW, with a pillion passenger, on any road including motorways
  • Test required: Theory test, then Mod 1 and Mod 2 practical tests on an A1-compliant bike

Schools that teach it, cheapest first:

  • Harley's Rider Training — from £565
  • Two Wheels Motorcycle Training — from £599

Edinburgh Motorcycle Training doesn't explicitly advertise A1 training separately — ask directly.

A2

Restricted licence

A2 is the middle step — a proper mid-size bike, with a route to upgrade to a full licence later without retaking your theory test.

  • Minimum age: 19
  • What it lets you do: Ride motorcycles up to 35kW (47bhp), power-to-weight ratio no more than 0.2kW/kg
  • Test required: Theory test (if you don't already hold one), then Mod 1 and Mod 2 on an A2-compliant bike
  • Progression: After 2 years on A2, upgrade to a full licence by passing Mod 1 and Mod 2 again on a bigger bike — no new theory test needed

Schools that teach it, cheapest first:

  • Harley's Rider Training — from £625
  • Two Wheels Motorcycle Training — from £649
Full A

Unrestricted licence

The top of the ladder — ride any motorcycle, with no power restriction.

  • Minimum age: 24 via Direct Access (DAS), or 21 if progressing from 2 years on A2
  • What it lets you do: Ride any motorcycle, no power restriction
  • Test required (Direct Access): Theory test, then Mod 1 and Mod 2 on a bike of at least 595cc and 40kW

Schools that teach it, cheapest first:

  • Edinburgh Motorcycle Training — from £699
  • Harley's Rider Training — from £719
  • Two Wheels Motorcycle Training — from £749
Mod 1 & 2

The practical test

Mod 1 and Mod 2 are the two-part practical test required for A1, A2 and full A licences — CBT doesn't need them.

  • Mod 1: Off-road manoeuvres — slow riding, slalom, U-turn, emergency stop, avoidance swerve
  • Mod 2: On-road riding, roughly 30–40 minutes, with the examiner following by radio

Most full-licence training includes Mod 1 & 2 preparation. Harley's Rider Training specifically highlights dedicated test-day coaching.
