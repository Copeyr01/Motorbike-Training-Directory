Privacy policy

Plain English, not legal jargon. Last updated July 2026.

Who we are

Upshift is an independent directory for motorbike training schools, currently covering Edinburgh. This policy explains what information we collect and what we do with it.

Information we collect

Upshift doesn't have user accounts or a review submission system yet, so right now we collect very little:

  • If you email us — for example to list your school or ask a question — we keep that email and its contents so we can reply.
  • If you search by postcode, that postcode is sent to postcodes.io, a free UK postcode lookup service, so we can show you the nearest schools. We don't store the postcode ourselves, and it isn't linked to you in any way.
  • We may collect basic, anonymous website usage data in future to understand how the site is used. We'll update this policy before that goes live if it isn't already.

Information about training schools

We publish information about motorbike training schools — names, addresses, contact details and licences taught — sourced from publicly available information. This is business information, not personal data about an individual, except where a school is run by a sole trader. If you run a school and want your listing corrected or removed, get in touch via our List your school page.

What we don't do

  • We don't sell your data.
  • We don't run ads or ad-tracking on this site.
  • We don't share your information with training schools without your consent.

Your rights

Under UK data protection law, you can ask what personal data we hold about you, ask us to correct it, or ask us to delete it. Email hello@upshiftuk.com and we'll respond.

Changes to this policy

We'll update this page as Upshift adds features — like reviews or accounts — that involve handling more personal data.

Contact

Questions about this policy: hello@upshiftuk.com

This is a plain-English policy written for an early-stage site with minimal data collection. It hasn't been reviewed by a solicitor — before Upshift adds features like accounts, payments or large-scale review collection, this policy should get a proper legal review.
